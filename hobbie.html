3D Printing

Most recently, Jackson Knight has taken up 3-d printing. Inspired by exosletons, he is currently working on a 3-d printed arm brace. His goal is to complete an entirely 3d printed exoskeleton that can fit around the human body. To accomplish this, he has learned about the anatomy of the human body and the different types of joints found in the arm. He has then replicated this in his arm model to provide the most flexiblity for wearers. He has also learned to 3-d model in Tinker-Cad and Fusion 360.

PC Building

Around 1 year ago, Jackson Knight had a dream to code and maintain his own local LLM models to research and develop his own AI models. However, he had an issue. All AI models require a computer to run, something he did not have. But this did not stop him. He learned to build a computer and found parts for cheap online. He then built his computer and devloped his own AI model called P.H.I.L.I.P. In his free time, Jackson plays games and 3-D models his prints on the computer.

Arduino

While developing his 3-D models, Jackson felt that there was something missing. He thought back to the movies and popular media that inspired this exoskeleton and realized that what he was missing was movement. He looked at the opening and closing mechanisms on the suits from Iron Man and the Power Armor from Fallout and saw that the exoskeltons all opened up to allow the wearer to easily step in. With this in mind, he researched different motors and micro controllers to control the motors. He then learned the Arduino language and the various libraries needed to achieve his dream.
