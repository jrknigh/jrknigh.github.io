Bacon Recipe

History of Bacon

Bacon's simplest form was first made in China as salted strips of pork belly. However, the name for bacon at the time meant all pork products. It wasn't until John Harris made the world's first commercial bacon processing plant in the 1700s in Calne, Wiltshire.

Ingredents

  • Thick cut Bacon
  • Brown sugar
  • Maple syrup
  • Cayenne pepper
  • Black pepper
  • Red Peper Flakes

Equipment Needed

  • Parchment paper
  • Baking sheet
  • Cooling rack

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to the temperature the bacon package says and then add brown sugar, black pepper, cayenne pepper, red pepper flake and maple syrup to a mixing bowl.
  2. Combine the sugar, syrup and spices until well combined. The mixture will be moist, but slightly lumpy.
  3. Cover a baking sheet with parchment paper, then lay out bacon strips in a single layer.
  4. Spread sugar, syrup and spice mixture out over the bacon, using your hand to pat it out as evenly as possible.
  5. Bake for 20 to 30 minutes until as crispy as you like, then transfer to a baking rack to cool slightly before serving.
